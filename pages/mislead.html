    Misleading Visualizations Reflection:


I chose this item to reflect on because it speaks to my brains natural interest in problem solving. Whenever I see a viz, after I take a look and understand what I am seeing, my first thought is how to make it better. So an assignment like identifying the misleading elements of a data viz made me really excited.

I learned a lot from this activity: the power of spatial alignment, meaningful labels, line charts that imply correlation, bin sizes, etc. This assignment really helped me when designing my own visualizations because I always questioned, "Am I using color correctly? Does putting these categories in this order imply something I don't want to imply? Are the labels clear enough so that even an inexperienced user can understand?"

I think the tedious nature of data visualization and the human brains process when observing visualizations is something that can be applied outside of the classroom as well. I am a very detail oriented person, so it makes sense that I am drawn to something so tedious. I like to make sure that my family plans are all made correctly, my hangouts with friends are timely, etc, and sometimes what I don't consider is the clarity of my communication. Even if I know how something will go, maybe there was something I communicated unclearly and misled a friend to meet me at the wrong place or be unavailable at the time our hangout was. Misleading vizes strangely apply to friends and family!
